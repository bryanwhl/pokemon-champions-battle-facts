# Source Policy

Use this hierarchy for factual claims:

1. Official Pokemon Champions in-game information, official Pokemon.com news/rules pages, Play! Pokemon rules documents, and Pokemon HOME Battle Data.
2. User-provided screenshots, exports, rental/team sheets, or battle logs, clearly labeled as user-provided evidence.
3. Canonical data APIs and codebases such as PokeAPI, Pokemon Showdown data, and @smogon/calc for main-series mechanics and Pokemon data.
4. Community/fan sites and social posts for leads only. Do not present them as authoritative unless cross-checked.

## Volatile Champions Facts

Treat these as current-date sensitive:

- Legal Pokemon roster and forms.
- Current regulation, bans, clauses, timer settings, and tournament format.
- In-game move availability and Champions-specific move descriptions.
- Usage statistics, common spreads, common items, and metagame rankings.
- Patch changes to status, mechanics, abilities, or moves.

If an official/current source is not available, say "I could not verify this for Pokemon Champions" and continue with the closest verified canonical data only if useful.

## Stable Facts

The 18-type chart is stable unless official Champions patch notes explicitly say otherwise. Pokemon species/form typing and base stats are stable for a named form in a named generation/source, but form names matter. For example, Rotom-Wash is not the same as Rotom, and Ogerpon forms differ.

## Required Wording

- Say "PokeAPI says..." for PokeAPI results.
- Say "@smogon/calc under Gen 9 assumptions gives..." for calculator output.
- Say "Champions legality unverified" when a move, Pokemon, form, or item has not been checked against current Champions data.
- Avoid vague phrases like "usually learns" or "should be legal" unless backed by a named source and version group.
